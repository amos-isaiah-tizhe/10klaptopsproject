Our Mission

The #10kLaptopsProject is an initiative dedicated to distributing 10,000 laptops to committed Nigerian youths, enabling productivity, education, and long-term career growth.

Our Story

The #10kLaptopsProject (now formally restructured under the Tech Access Foundation for Youth Empowerment) is a youth-focused initiative led by Dr. Ayilola Ayotomiwa ( @silverpenydr ) to bridge the digital divide in Nigeria by providing laptops and tech skills training to young people.

This project began organically in early November 2025 as a personal vision from Ayilola, without a pre-planned roadmap or major funding. It stemmed purely from compassion and conviction to help underserved Nigerian youths access technology...

1. Mission

The core mission is to empower 10,000 Nigerian youths (with a long-term vision to potentially expand globally) by equipping them with laptops and in-demand tech/digital skills. This aims to:

  • Create access to tools and opportunities in the digital economy.
  • Boost education, productivity, creativity, freelancing, and career growth.
  • Improve Nigeria's human development index by reducing barriers like lack of devices.
  • Help lower issues such as unemployment, crime, and drug abuse among youth through purposeful skill-building and self-reliance.

The project emphasizes sustainable impact over one-off giveaways—pairing hardware with verified training and commitment—so recipients can use the laptops productively for years. Ayilola has described it as a movement "born purely out of compassion and a genuine desire to empower Nigerian youths," with the ultimate goal of creating multiple pathways to success and dignity for young people.

In his words: "Equip 10k Nigerian youth with 10k laptops" over a five-year period. Aspire to "distribute over 1,000 laptops and empower 2,000 Nigerian youths with in-demand tech skills" in the near term (e.g., 2026 goals). Focus on "bringing life, purpose, and impact to everything I touch" while opening doors through tech.

2. How It Started

The project began organically in early November 2025 as a personal vision from Ayilola, without a pre-planned roadmap or major funding. It stemmed purely from compassion and conviction to help underserved Nigerian youths access technology.

Key timeline of the launch:

  • Ayilola first publicly brainstormed the idea on X by tweeting about providing 10,000 laptops to 10,000 youths.
  • He reflected privately and discussed options like installment plans with vendors (e.g., Val Gadgets), but prioritized genuine empowerment.
  • He decided to reach out to celebrities/philanthropists for support and shared the vision publicly, tagging figures like Don Jazzy (@DONJAZZY).
  • The major breakthrough came quickly: Don Jazzy donated ₦20 million in response, which formalized the effort and brought total early donations to over ₦21 million.
  • Distributions started almost immediately with small batches, transparent receipts, and team involvement.

Ayilola has repeatedly shared: "The 10K Laptops for 10K Nigerian Youths initiative began just a few days ago, without any pre-planned roadmap. It was a movement born purely out of compassion... I simply had a strong conviction to start."

It quickly grew from a solo idea into a community-backed project with a small dedicated team.

3. How It Works

The project operates with high transparency, accountability, and a structured selection/distribution process to ensure real impact:

  • Funding: Primarily through public donations (major early one from Don Jazzy), with ongoing calls for support. Funds are tracked publicly via screenshots and breakdowns.
  • Beneficiary Selection: Applications open periodically (often tied to training cohorts). Priority given to serious, committed youths—especially those completing free/paid tech training programs (e.g., via Nigerians in the Middle East Community — NIME cohorts in cybersecurity, data analysis, UI/UX, web dev, graphic design, video editing, etc.).
  • Training + Laptop Pairing: Laptops are awarded as rewards/tools to those who demonstrate dedication in skill-building, ensuring devices are used productively.
  • Procurement & Delivery: Laptops sourced from trusted vendors (e.g., Val Gadgets, Neo Official, Spartan Solutions)—typically reliable mid-range mod els like Dell Latitude or HP EliteBook (8th Gen i5, 8GB+ RAM, 256GB SSD, ~₦250,000–260,000 each including delivery). Purchases are made in batches, with public receipts shared.
  • Governance: Now formally under the Tech Access Foundation for Youth Empowerment (NGO structure) for better long-term sustainability and accountability.
  • Timeline: Planned as a 5-year initiative to allow gradual, responsible scaling rather than rushed mass distribution.

The emphasis is on mutual value—laptops go to youths who will maximize them, while donors see tangible, documented results.

4. Community Impact

The project has already created measurable, positive ripple effects in the Nigerian youth community:

  • Direct Beneficiaries: Over 47 laptops distributed (in verified batches: e.g., 13 → 24 → 34 cumulative by late 2025, plus smaller recent ones), enabling recipients to pursue freelancing, studies, content creation, remote work, and tech careers.
  • Skill Empowerment: Hundreds more youths engaged through linked training cohorts (NIME and others), gaining marketable skills even if not yet receiving a laptop.
  • Hope & Motivation: Testimonials from beneficiaries show renewed purpose—many who felt "hopeless" now believe in their potential. Public stories highlight transformations in productivity and confidence.
  • Broader Awareness: Sparked conversations on youth empowerment, digital access, and philanthropy in Nigeria. It inspired trust in transparent initiatives (with receipts and updates countering skepticism).
  • Social Good: Aims to improve human development, reduce unemployment-related issues, and build a more skilled generation. Ayilola notes it helps "talented youths who only needed a little push now believe again."
  • Community Building: Fostered a supportive network on X—volunteers, team members (@Horiyourme, @boye4christ2006, @mobola_ade), supporters, and beneficiaries amplify the hashtag and share wins.

While still early (with ~₦8–9 million remaining from initial funds and more needed for the full 10,000 goal), the project demonstrates scalable, accountable impact—one laptop and one skill at a time—earning praise for its genuine, youth-centered approach.
